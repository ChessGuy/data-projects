Academic Background

I have a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Penn State University and an M.B.A. from the Jack Welch Management Institute of Strayer University. I am also a Professional Engineer in the state of West Virginia, a Project Management Professional, a Six Sigma Black Belt, and a Certified Scrum Master.

Career Aspirations

I am aspiring to work in Data Science, and I would love to find a role that works well with my academic background. For example, I could be very successful in the financial industry since I have an M.B.A. and many years managing project money. Additionally, I clearly have an interest in technology and engineering so working as an AI engineer for automating a manufacturing process could also be good. I see the potential AI has to solve a lot of problems, and I want to be part of that effort.

Professional Interests

I have a background in several different technical areas, and my problem (or strength) is that I have enjoyed pretty much all of them. I really enjoyed data analysis and root cause analysis because it is like solving a mystery. I love problem solving, so I also enjoyed my work as a technical services engineer because there was always something new to learn. And while I don't do that anymore, the experience I gained has made me a successful project manager.

Personal Introduction

I love music and I am well versed in several different instruments. I primarily play piano and guitar, but I have previously played clarinet, saxophone, harmonica, bass guitar, ukulele, and even ocarina. In college, I founded the first Penn State Guitar Club. I have played in a church band, and I have taught one of my friend's daughters how to play piano.

My other major hobby is gaming of all kinds, which is why I chose to do my capstone project on board games. In my local community, I have started a group that plays chess and one that plays bridge at my local library. I frequently play online board games with friends, and I have a large collection myself. I also play a lot of different video games, and I tend to like the ones that reward strategic thinking the most.
